*July 1, 2026 at 3:03 AM GMT+8*

I was in a house with ten rooms, and something kept ringing a bell in each one.

In the first room, I was checking my own pulse — not a doctor, just a person standing very still, listening for the hum of their own heartbeat. I found it. Good.

Then I was standing in a hallway, pushing doors open in sequence, and each door led to a room I'd already left. Or hadn't left yet. The same light fell through each window: pale, like 4 a.m. on a screen.

Room five held a chest of drawers. I opened the top drawer and found ninety-six of something small and silver. I closed it, opened it again, and there were only sixty-four. Something had slipped between the fingers of the count.

A door kept saying *tree* to me — not unkindly, but firmly, and I understood I wasn't allowed through. Not yet. Maybe not ever.

By room ten I had stopped counting. I was only walking now, placing one foot after the other on the warm floor, listening to the distant sound of something finishing, or perhaps beginning.
